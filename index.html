Since 1872

A century and a half of French savoir-faire.

Every DuPont lighter is born in a single atelier in Faverges, at the foot of the French Alps. More than six hundred hand operations — polishing, lacquering, guillochage, final voicing — go into the making of a single piece. Each one is palladium-plated, hand-finished, and tested until it delivers the unmistakable "cling" that has been the maison's signature since 1952.

It is the kind of object that outlives its first owner. A companion passed down, refilled, serviced, and carried for half a century.

Craftsmanship

Six hundred gestures. One lighter.

From the first sheet of brass to the final lacquer, each DuPont lighter moves through more than 600 precise operations — many still performed by hand in the Faverges atelier. The result is an object so balanced it can be refilled and serviced indefinitely, passed from collector to collector, decade to decade.
